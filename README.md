# Title
Clinical Trial Analysis of a Diabetes Medication: Efficacy and Safety evaluation.

# Project Overview
This project analyses clinical trial data to eveluate the effectiveness and safety of a new diabetes medication. Using statistical analysis and  data visualization techniques, the study assesses treatment impact on HbA1v levels, patient response patterns, and potential safety risks.

# Objectives
1. Determine whether the medication significantly reduces HbA1c levels compared to placebo.
2. Assess variations in treatment effectiveness across patient groups.
3. Evaluate the safety profile based on adverse events.
4. Identify relationships between clinical variables and treatment outcomes.

# Tools & Technologies
Python|pandas|numpy|matplotlib|seaborn|scipy|statsmodels|scikit-learn|JupyterNotebook.

# Data Preparation
1. Clean and drop missing values
2. Convert variables to appropriate data type
3. Applied encoding for categorical variables (group_treatment)
4. Validated dataset for consistency and usability

 # Visual Analysis and Insights
 1. HbA1c Trends Over Time: Treatment group shows a consistent decline, indicating improved glycaemic control.
 2.  Treatment Vs Placebo: Lower median HbA1c in treatment group confirms effectiveness.
 3.  Adverse Events Distribution: Identifies the most frequent side effects for monitoring.
 4.  Baseline Vs Change: Higher initial HbA1c  is associated with  greater reduction
 5.  Age Group Analysis: Suggests variation in treatment effectiveness across demographics.

 # Statistical Analysis
 1. T-Test: Significant difference between treatment and placebo group (p< 0.05).
 2. ANOVA: Time and Treatment both significantly influence HbA1c outcomes.
 3. Chi-square Test: chi=3.453, p= 0.3269 relationship between Treatment and Adverse Events
 4. Correlation Analysis: Examines relationship between disease duration and treatment response.

# Key Findings
1. The medication significantly reduces HbA1c levels.
2. Treatment effects improve over time.
3. Patients with higher baseline HbA1c benefit most
4. Effectiveness varies across patient groups
5. The safety profile is favourable

# Safety Analysis
The chi-square test indicates  the adverse events  are not significantly associated with the treament, suggesting a favourable safety profile.

# Implications
1. The medication is a viable option for improving glycaemic control.
2. Personalised treatment strategies may enhance outcomes.
3. Safety findings support clinical decision-making.
4. Further research is required for long-term validation.

# Recommendations
1. Consider adoption in clinical settings for eligible patients.
2. Apply patient-specific treatment strategies.
3. Continue monitoring adverse events.
4. Conduct further long-term and large-scale studies.

# Conclusion
The analysis demonstrates that the medication is effective in reducing HbA1c levels and shows a favourable safety profile. These findings support its potential clinical value and highlight the importance of data-driven approaches in healthcare decision-making.
